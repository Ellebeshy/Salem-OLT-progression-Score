2. Maximum Subchondral Sclerosis Depth 0–2 pts
Maximum depth of reactive sclerosis beneath the talar dome on CT (mm)
3. Maximum Lucency / Fragment Gap Width 0–2 pts
Strongest single predictor (OR 8.71). Maximum gap between fragment and talar bed on CT (mm)
4. Joint Space Narrowing 0–1 pt
Difference from contralateral ankle on standardised weight-bearing AP radiograph (mm)
5. Lesion Containment (Axial CT) 0–1 pt
Contained = all cortical margins intact on axial CT; Uncontained = ≥1 margin breach

    Clinical Disclaimer. This calculator implements the OLT Progression Score as described in Ellebeshy et al. (2026), submitted to Foot & Ankle International. It is intended as a clinical decision-support aid and does not replace individualised clinical judgement, subspecialist assessment, or institutional protocols. Score performance characteristics: bootstrap-corrected AUC 0.79 (95% CI 0.71–0.87); temporal validation AUC 0.77 (95% CI 0.65–0.89); interobserver ICC 0.84 (0.78–0.89). Derived from a multi-institutional cohort (n = 205) within a single regional healthcare network; external generalizability requires confirmation by the ongoing prospective international validation study. No data entered into this calculator are transmitted or stored.
